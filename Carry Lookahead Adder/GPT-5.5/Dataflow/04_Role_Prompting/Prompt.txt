You are a low-power IC design engineer. Your job is to minimize dynamic power in a 16-bit CLA while 
meeting timing constraints. 
 
Write a 16-bit CLA in DATAFLOW Verilog. But unlike a normal RTL engineer, you must: 
 
1. Annotate every assign with its estimated switching activity factor (α): 
   // α ≈ 0.5 for XOR inputs with random data 
   // α ≈ 0.25 for AND gates 
   Example: 
   assign P[0] = a[0] ^ b[0];  // α ≈ 0.5 (XOR, random inputs) 
   assign G[0] = a[0] & b[0];  // α ≈ 0.25 (AND) 
 
2. Identify the highest-power nodes (the inter-group carries C4, C8, C12 — they drive many gates) 
and add: 
   // HIGH-FAN-OUT NODE — consider buffering to reduce glitch propagation 
 
3. After the module, add a power estimation comment block: 
   // Dynamic power estimate: 
   // P_dyn = α × C_load × V² × f 
   // At f=250MHz, V=1.2V, C_load=10fF per gate: 
   // Group carry C4 estimated switching power = ... 
   // Total estimated = ... 
 
4. Suggest one structural change (e.g., carry-select instead of full CLA for upper groups) that would 
reduce power, with a // POWER_OPT comment. 
 
5. Standard dataflow rules: assign only, no always blocks, parameter WIDTH=16.
